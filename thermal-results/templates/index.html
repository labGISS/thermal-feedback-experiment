Statistics Guide

Experiment Design

Normal condition (experiment 1)
The participant touches the thermal device and reports which finger(s) feel warm, with no secondary task. Baseline measure of thermal localization ability.
Dual-Task condition (experiment 2)
Same thermal task performed simultaneously with a 2-back cognitive task. Measures whether cognitive load degrades thermal perception.
Trial
A single stimulus event: 1, 2, or 3 fingers are heated and the participant selects which fingers they felt. Each condition has 7 trials.
Fingers
Index (finger 0), Middle (finger 1), Ring (finger 2). These are the three fingers instrumented with thermal actuators.

Accuracy Metrics

Exact Match (%)
Percentage of trials where the set of fingers reported by the participant exactly matches the set of fingers that were heated. The strictest accuracy measure — even one extra or missed finger counts as wrong.
Per-Finger Hit Rate (%)
For each individual finger that was heated, was it reported? Aggregated across all fingers and trials. Less strict than Exact Match — partial credit for getting some fingers right.
TP — True Positive
A heated finger that the participant correctly reported as felt. Good outcome.
FP — False Positive
A finger reported as felt that was not heated. The participant perceived a sensation that wasn't there (false alarm).
FN — False Negative
A heated finger that the participant did not report. A missed stimulus.
Hit Rate per finger
Of all the trials where a specific finger was heated, how often did the participant report feeling it? 100% means perfect detection for that finger.
False Alarm Rate (FAR)
Of all the trials where a finger was not heated, how often did the participant wrongly report feeling it? Lower is better. A high FAR suggests the participant guessed or the neighbouring finger's heat spread.

Discrimination

Single-Finger Discrimination
On trials where only one finger was heated, did the participant identify the correct finger? This isolates pure localization ability from pattern detection.
Confusion Matrix
Rows = which finger was actually heated. Columns = which finger the participant reported. Diagonal cells (blue) are correct identifications. Off-diagonal cells (red) are confusions — e.g. Index heated but Middle reported.

Timing & Subjective Ratings

Response Time — RT (ms)
Time in milliseconds from when the participant touched the device to when they submitted their response. Shorter RT may indicate higher certainty, but very short RT can also signal impulsive responding.
Clarity (1–5)
Participant's self-rated clarity of the thermal sensation: 1 = very unclear, 5 = very clear. Indicates perceived signal quality independent of objective accuracy.
Temperature Estimate (1–5)
Participant's perceived intensity of the temperature change on a 1–5 scale. Not a calibrated temperature; a subjective magnitude judgment.

2-Back Cognitive Task

2-Back task
A working-memory task where the participant must indicate whether the current stimulus matches the one that appeared two positions earlier in the sequence. Widely used to impose cognitive load.
Hit Rate (2-back)
Percentage of actual target matches correctly detected by the participant. High hit rate = good working memory performance under load.
Commission errors (Wrong)
Responses given when there was no actual match — false alarms in the 2-back task.
Missed targets
Actual matches that the participant did not respond to — omission errors.

Post-Session Questionnaire

Overall Comfort (1–5)
How comfortable the participant felt during the whole session. 1 = very uncomfortable, 5 = very comfortable.
Perceived Intensity (1–5)
General perceived strength of the thermal stimulation across the session.
2-Back Difficulty (1–5)
How difficult the participant found the 2-back cognitive task. 1 = very easy, 5 = very hard.
